Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K)

These nutrients are essential for crop growth. Use soil test kits or lab analysis to determine levels.

How to Measure?

  • Use Soil Test Kits (Available online or in stores).
  • Send soil samples to a Soil Testing Lab for accurate results.
  • Try DIY methods using color-changing test strips.

Measuring Soil pH

pH affects how plants absorb nutrients. Most crops prefer a pH between 6.0 and 7.5.

How to Measure?

  • Use a pH Meter (Insert probe into moist soil).
  • Test with pH Strips (Mix soil with water and dip a strip).
  • DIY Method: Use vinegar and baking soda to check acidity/alkalinity.

Measuring Rainfall

Rainfall affects crop yield. Use weather stations or manual rain gauges to track it.

How to Measure?

  • Use a Rain Gauge (Collect and measure rainwater in mm).
  • Check weather websites for accurate data.
  • DIY: Place a straight-sided container in an open area and measure collected rain.

Check Rainfall Data

Measuring Soil Moisture

Soil moisture affects seed germination and root development. Maintaining optimal moisture is critical for plant health.

How to Measure?

  • Use a Soil Moisture Sensor (Electronic probes give real-time data).
  • Try the Feel and Appearance Method (Take a soil sample and squeeze it).
  • DIY: Insert a dry wooden stick into the soil for a few hours; check for wet marks.

Identifying Soil Type

The soil type determines water retention, root penetration, and nutrient availability. Common types include sandy, clayey, loamy, and silt.

How to Identify?

  • Jar Test: Mix soil and water in a jar, shake, and let settle. Layers reveal texture.
  • Touch Test: Rub moist soil between fingers—sandy feels gritty, clayey feels sticky.
  • Color & Structure: Loamy soil is dark, crumbly; sandy is pale and loose.
